Colonial Heritage to High-Speed Rail

Dari Warisan Kolonial ke Kereta Cepat

Chronological overview of the development of Indonesia's railway network, starting in 1864 under the Dutch East Indies to the modern HSR era.

Tinjauan kronologis perkembangan jaringan rel kereta api Indonesia, dimulai tahun 1864 di bawah Hindia Belanda hingga era HSR modern.

1864

The First Spadework

Pancangan Rel Pertama

Governor-General L.A.J.W. Baron Sloet van de Beele broke ground on the first railway line in Kemijen, Semarang. Constructed by Dutch private company NISM, the Semarang-Tanggung line served agricultural logistics.

Gubernur Jenderal L.A.J.W. Baron Sloet van de Beele memancang rel pertama di Kemijen, Semarang. Dibangun oleh perusahaan swasta Belanda NISM, jalur Semarang-Tanggung ini melayani logistik komoditas perkebunan.

1925

Electrified Rail Debut

Elektrifikasi Pertama

Dutch colonial authorities electrified the Batavia (Jakarta) to Tanjung Priok line. This marked the birth of regional commuter systems in the capital, laying the groundwork for the modern KRL commuter line.

Pemerintah kolonial meluncurkan lokomotif listrik pertama yang menghubungkan Batavia (Jakarta) ke Tanjung Priok. Tonggak ini menandai lahirnya sistem kereta komuter perkotaan pertama di Batavia.

1945

Nationalization (DKARI)

Nasionalisasi Aset

Following independence, Indonesian rail workers seized headquarters from Japanese occupational forces on Sept 28, forming DKARI (now PT KAI). Sept 28 is celebrated annually as National Railway Day.

Pasca proklamasi, angkatan muda perkeretaapian merebut kantor pusat perkeretaapian dari militer Jepang pada 28 September, membentuk DKARI (cikal bakal PT KAI). Hari ini diperingati sebagai Hari Kereta Api.

2010

The Service Revolution

Revolusi Layanan KAI

Led by Ignasius Jonan, KAI underwent major structural reforms: eliminating roof riders, introducing e-ticketing, air-conditioning all passenger cars, and transforming railway stations into clean, commercial hubs.

Di bawah kepemimpinan Ignasius Jonan, KAI merombak total pelayanan: menghapus penumpang atap kereta, menerapkan e-ticketing, AC di seluruh gerbong, dan menata stasiun menjadi kawasan bersih dan tertib.

2023

Enter the HSR Era

Era Kereta Cepat Mulai

Indonesia launched "Whoosh" (KCJB), connecting Jakarta and Bandung. It represents the Southern Hemisphere's first high-speed rail line operating at 350 km/h, reducing travel times from 3 hours to 36 minutes.

Indonesia resmi meluncurkan "Whoosh" (KCJB), menghubungkan Jakarta dan Bandung. Menjadi jaringan kereta cepat pertama di Belahan Bumi Selatan dengan kecepatan operasional mencapai 350 km/jam.

2026

Surabaya Phase Approved

Persetujuan Fase Surabaya

President Prabowo Subianto granted principal approval to initiate detailed engineering design studies for the Jakarta-Surabaya HSR extension, targeting full funding through public-private partnerships.

Presiden Prabowo Subianto memberikan persetujuan prinsip untuk memulai studi detail rancang bangun (DED) perpanjangan HSR Jakarta-Surabaya, dengan skema pendanaan KPBU.

2045

RIPNAS Golden Vision

Visi Emas RIPNAS

Under the National Railway Master Plan, Indonesia aims to expand rail networks to 37,000–60,000 km, fully linking Sumatra, Sulawesi, Kalimantan, and urban centers with standard gauge and high-speed rail lines.

Sesuai Rencana Induk Perkeretaapian Nasional, Indonesia menargetkan panjang jalur rel mencapai 37.000–60.000 km, menghubungkan Sumatera, Sulawesi, Kalimantan, dan pusat kota.

Ridership Performance & Dynamics

Kinerja Volume Penumpang & Dinamika

Since its commercial launch in late 2023, Whoosh has scaled operations to 62 daily trips. In 2025, the network carried over 6.2 million passengers. KAI and KCIC implemented a dynamic pricing algorithm (ranging from IDR 175,000 to IDR 600,000) to balance demand between off-peak weekdays and weekend holiday rushes. The peak ridership record was achieved on June 27, 2025, reaching 26,770 passengers in a single day.

Sejak peluncuran komersial akhir 2023, Whoosh meningkatkan perjalanan hingga 62 perjalanan per hari. Sepanjang 2025, jaringan ini mengangkut lebih dari 6,2 juta penumpang. KAI dan KCIC menerapkan tarif dinamis (IDR 175.000 hingga IDR 600.000) untuk menyeimbangkan okupansi hari kerja dan akhir pekan. Rekor tertinggi diraih pada 27 Juni 2025, dengan 26.770 penumpang dalam sehari.

Trans-Sumatra Railway Grid

Jaringan Rel Trans-Sumatera

The ambitious Trans-Sumatra initiative is designed to link Aceh at the northern tip with Lampung in the south over a ~2,700 km network. Rather than high-speed passenger services, the focus remains on upgrading regional legacy tracks and connecting coal, palm oil, and raw resource freight logistics to container ports.

Inisiatif Trans-Sumatera dirancang untuk menghubungkan Aceh di ujung utara hingga Lampung di selatan dengan total panjang ~2.700 km. Fokus utama proyek ini adalah perbaikan jalur rel konvensional untuk mendukung logistik batubara, kelapa sawit, dan hasil komoditas bumi menuju pelabuhan kontainer.

Priority sections include the Banda Aceh-Besitang line reactivation (478 km) and state-backed investments under BUMN consolidations to expand freight carrying capacities.

Segmen prioritas yang kini dikerjakan mencakup reaktivasi Banda Aceh-Besitang (478 km) serta optimalisasi angkutan barang batubara sumatera bagian selatan.

Makassar–Parepare Pioneer Line

Jalur Perintis Makassar–Parepare

Sulawesi's first railway line opened for commercial operations in October 2022. Spanning 142 km from Makassar to Parepare, this standard-gauge line serves both passenger trains and cement logistics transport for local industrial plants. It represents the initial phase of the Trans-Sulawesi rail vision.

Jalur kereta api perdana Sulawesi resmi beroperasi komersial pada Oktober 2022. Membentang 142 km dari Makassar ke Parepare, jalur rel standard-gauge ini melayani mobilitas komuter warga serta logistik semen dari pabrik Tonasa ke pelabuhan.

IKN Metropolitan Connectivity

Konektivitas Metropolitan IKN

With the construction of Indonesia's new capital Nusantara (IKN), planners have outlined a 2,772 km network linking East, South, and West Kalimantan. The initial construction focuses on passenger airport links and regional freight corridors between Balikpapan and Samarinda. No physical tracks have been laid, but preliminary land clearances are underway.

Seiring pembangunan Ibu Kota Nusantara (IKN), pemerintah merancang total 2.772 km jalur rel di Kalimantan. Rencana awal berfokus pada konektivitas bandara IKN serta jalur logistik Balikpapan-Samarinda. Proyek ini masih berada pada tahap pembebasan lahan awal dan detail perencanaan rute.

Bali Urban Subway & LRT

Subway Urban & LRT Bali

To alleviate gridlock in tourist corridors, Bali broke ground on an urban subway network in September 2024. Designed to link Ngurah Rai Airport with Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur, the project targets underground drilling to preserve cultural aesthetics and avoid complex above-ground land acquisition conflicts. However, financial restructuring in early 2026 has slowed construction progress.

Untuk mengatasi kemacetan parah di koridor wisata, Bali memulai groundbreaking proyek kereta bawah tanah (Subway) pada September 2024. Dirancang menghubungkan Bandara Ngurah Rai dengan Kuta, Seminyak, dan Sanur, rekayasa bawah tanah dipilih untuk menghindari konflik pembebasan lahan permukaan.

Strategic Goals of RIPNAS 2045

Sasaran Strategis RIPNAS 2045

The National Railway Master Plan (RIPNAS) targets a modal share shift to boost passenger rail to 7-9% of national travel and rail freight to 11-13% of domestic logistics by 2045. To support this, Bappenas outlines a cumulative investment requirement of Rp 1,200 Trillion over two decades, prioritizing inter-city double tracking and standard-gauge HSR trunk lines.

Rencana Induk Perkeretaapian Nasional (RIPNAS) menargetkan peningkatan pangsa moda angkutan kereta api penumpang menjadi 7-9% dan angkutan barang logistik kereta api menjadi 11-13% pada tahun 2045. Guna mencapai target tersebut, Bappenas memproyeksikan kebutuhan investasi total mencapai Rp 1.200 Triliun selama dua dekade.

  • Complete double-tracking across Sumatra's industrial belt.
  • Menyelesaikan jalur rel ganda (double-track) di kawasan industri Sumatera.
  • Implement standard gauge networks in IKN and Sulawesi.
  • Menerapkan jaringan rel standard-gauge di IKN dan Sulawesi.
  • Establish high-speed trunk link connecting Java coast-to-coast.
  • Membangun koridor utama kereta cepat (HSR) yang menghubungkan ujung barat hingga ujung timur Jawa.
