Algorithm: Other High-Threat Neurologic & Systemic Toxins

PATIENT PRESENTS WITH ATYPICAL SYMPTOMS

Rapid neurotoxicity, delayed organ failure, etc.

IDENTIFY TOXIDROME

Match clinical picture to likely toxin.

MUSHROOM (AMATOXIN)

SYMPTOMS

Severe V/D 6-24h post-ingestion, followed by a 'honeymoon' phase, then acute liver & renal failure.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

  • Aggressive fluid resuscitation
  • Contact Poison Control / Toxicology
  • Activated charcoal if within 1-2h
  • Monitor LFTs, renal function, glucose
  • Silibinin / N-acetylcysteine per protocol
  • Prepare for liver transplant consult
PARALYTIC SHELLFISH POISONING (PSP)

SYMPTOMS

Rapid onset (30-60 min). Perioral numbness/tingling, ataxia, cranial nerve dysfunction, ascending paralysis, respiratory failure.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

  • AIRWAY PROTECTION IS PRIORITY
  • Low threshold for intubation
  • Intensive supportive care in ICU
  • No specific antitoxin exists
  • Activated charcoal may be considered
  • Recovery usually within days with support
