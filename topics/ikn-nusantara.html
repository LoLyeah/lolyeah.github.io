Context

Why Relocate the Capital?

The move from Jakarta to East Kalimantan is driven by three main environmental and economic factors.

🌊

Jakarta's Ecological Crisis

Jakarta is sinking at rates of up to 15cm annually in some northern coastal areas. Combined with seasonal flooding, rising sea levels, and severe traffic congestion costing $4.5B/year, relocation is an ecological necessity.

⚖️

Economic Decentralization

Java constitutes only 7% of Indonesia's landmass but accounts for 58% of national GDP and 56% of the population. Moving the capital shifts the economic center of gravity toward the geographical heart of the archipelago.

🌲

Sustainable Forest City

Built from scratch, Nusantara is designed to run on 100% renewable energy, preserve 65% of its land as protected forest canopy, and establish a green urban development standard for global tropical cities.

Completed ▼

    Presidential Complex Completed Istana Negara, Istana Garuda, Ceremonial Field, and Secretariat. Officially hosted the 79th Independence Day in 2024.
  • ✓

  • ✓
    Key Public Buildings Completed initial Coordinating Ministry (Kemenko) offices, minister housing, and basic ASN apartments.
  • ✓
    Logistics Port & Supply Chain Built dedicated KIPP logistics port in Sepaku and supply corridors to support heavy transport and construction materials.
Phase II: Ecosystem & Political Capital 2025 – 2029
Active ▼

Focus on transition of legislative and judicial functions, expansion of housing networks, public transit, hospitals, and global investments.

  • ⚡
    Legislative & Judicial Complexes Building 16 legislative buildings (41.81ha) and 4 judicial buildings (15.15ha) for DPR, MPR, MA, and MK. Designs approved by President Prabowo.
  • ⚡
    Toll Roads & Transit Infrastructure Remaining toll road segments nearing completion (88–96% complete, mid-2026). The Autonomous Rail Transit (ART) pilot units were returned to China after the 2024 proof-of-concept trials concluded the system required manual overrides and did not meet full autonomy standards — a revised transport masterplan for BRT and feeder services was finalized in mid-2025.
  • ⚡
    Healthcare & Education Ecosystem Three hospitals are operating in KIPP: RSUP Nusantara, Mayapada Hospital Nusantara, and Hermina Hospital Nusantara. Higher-education partnerships are expanding under the 2025–2045 education roadmap. SMA Taruna Nusantara welcomed the first 191 students present on campus on 9 July 2026, with additional students scheduled to follow.
  • ⚡
    Smart City Technology Deployment Smart poles installed throughout KIPP, each integrated with CCTV, air quality sensors, and vehicle flow analytics. An integrated digital city dashboard monitors urban operations, energy use, and environmental metrics in real-time — part of the city's six-component smart city framework.
  • ⚡
    Civil Servant Housing Blocks (ASN Towers) Completing all 47 towers of ASN and defense staff apartments, equipped with smart water meters and centralized waste sorting.
Phase III: Urban Maturation & Mass Transit 2030 – 2034
Upcoming ▼

Scaling Nusantara's livability with full-scale transit expansions, industry cluster setups, and utility completions.

  • Airport Express Connectivity Completing high-speed transit line connecting KIPP to Sepinggan Airport (Balikpapan) in under 50 minutes.
  • Water & Waste Operability Reaching 100% operations on full-scale IPAM (drinking water) and IPAL (wastewater treatment) systems across the city.
  • Clean Industry Launch Initializing smart pharmaceutical, green tech, and agriculture processing factories to fuel municipal GDP.
  • Security & Defense Command Headquarters Establishing smart military (TNI) and police (Polri) headquarters, integrating AI-driven border control and cyber security commands.
  • Economic Cluster Expansion Accelerating realized capacity in designated clusters like clean tech assembly, ecotourism, and regional supply distribution.
Phase IV: Social Resilience & Education 2035 – 2039
Upcoming ▼

Integration of local communities, expansion of education, and growth of three-city ecosystem.

  • Education & Health Ecosystem Complete deployment of top universities, advanced laboratories, specialized clinics, and medical research campuses.
  • Three-City Integration Harmonizing trade, labor, and transport between Samarinda, Balikpapan, and the Core Nusantara Capital City (KIKN).
  • Economic Diversification & Eco-tourism Launching regional high-tech parks, smart logistics hubs, and international eco-tourism zones linked to nature reserves.
  • Social-Cultural Heritage & Integration Establishing cultural museums, preserving indigenous forest community centers (Mentawir), and integrating local wisdom into civic frameworks.
Phase V: The Centennial Completion 2040 – 2045
Upcoming ▼

Nusantara reaches maturity, establishing itself as a global icon of ecological urbanization for Indonesia Emas 2045.

  • Net Zero and 100% Renewable Achieving carbon neutrality. Electricity grids supplied by 100% green sources; combustion engines banned in favor of EVs.
  • Global Liveability Top-10 Realizing the '10-minute city' layout. 1.9M population enjoying high-speed connectivity, green spaces, and clean air.
  • Inter-city High-Speed Rail Constructing high-speed passenger rail networks connecting KIPP directly to Samarinda and Balikpapan city centers.
  • Global Connectivity & Digital Hub Activating Nusantara's international business district, launching global digital partnerships, and ranking among world top smart cities.

Latest Updates

Recent Key Developments (2025–2026)

Recent legislative changes, executive approvals, and judicial rulings that shaped Nusantara's trajectory this year.

Mid-2026

Constitutional Court Capital Ruling

The Constitutional Court (MK) clarified that Jakarta officially remains the capital city of Indonesia until a formal presidential decree (Keppres) translates the capital status to Nusantara. Construction and budgeting continue legally during this dual transitional status.

Late 2025

Perpres No. 79/2025 Reaffirmation

Presidential Regulation No. 79 of 2025 updated the 2025 Government Work Plan and reaffirmed a policy target for Nusantara to become Indonesia's political capital in 2028, supported by phased ASN relocation and infrastructure delivery.

Early 2026

Vice Presidential Working Site

Logistics and support networks are deployed to allow the Vice President and selected executive staff to work directly from administrative buildings in IKN, encouraging ministerial shifts.

FY 2026

APBN 2026 Allocation & Phase II Contract Surge

Phase II procurement accelerated through late 2025. By 17 December, OIKN reported that 26 of 28 construction and supervision packages had been signed, including work supporting legislative and judicial precincts targeted for 2027–2028 completion.

June 2025

Nusantara International Airport Certified

Nusantara International Airport received its official certification from the Ministry of Transportation in June 2025 and has been operational under a “special status” — serving government operations, Air Force missions, and charter flights. Full commercial passenger service is targeted for 2026, potentially transforming access to the new capital from major cities.

Nov 2024 – Now

Basuki Hadimuljono Leads OIKN

Former Minister of Public Works Basuki Hadimuljono was appointed Head of the IKN Authority (Kepala Otorita IKN) on November 5, 2024 by President Prabowo Subianto. His engineering background and deep familiarity with Phase I construction have positioned him to oversee Phase II’s legislative/judicial complex builds. In July 2026, he proposed an increased Rp 15 trillion budget for 2027 to accelerate key milestones.

Feb 2026

US Smart City Grant & South Korea SCCC

The U.S. government announced a US$2.49 million grant in February 2026 to support IKN’s enterprise architecture, smart city planning, and investment ecosystem. Separately, a Indonesia–South Korea Smart City Cooperation Center (SCCC) broke ground in IKN in mid-2026 to accelerate knowledge transfer, technology adoption, and institutional capacity building for urban digital services.

Risk Audit

Key Challenges & Roadblocks

While construction advances, IKN faces administrative, structural, and economic headwinds.

“Ghost Town” Narrative & Population Gap

A high-profile 2025 Guardian investigation warned of IKN becoming a "glorified ghost town." While the broader Nusantara region registered ~150,000 residents in 2025, the core KIPP zone itself hosts only ~2,000 civil servants plus ~8,000 construction workers — far below targets. Government officials, including VP Gibran, dispute the label, pointing to phased relocation timelines.

Investor Chicken-and-Egg Dilemma

A critical circular trap: investors demand dense population and services before deploying commercial capital, but residents need commercial infrastructure to justify moving. Realized private investment reached Rp 72.39T by mid-2026 — substantial but far short of the Rp 225T in LoI commitments. SoftBank’s $100B withdrawal in 2022 epitomizes the risk perception challenge.

Budget Cuts & Fiscal Prioritization

State budget for IKN has been slashed steeply: from Rp 43.4T in 2024, to Rp 13T in 2025, to Rp 6.3T in 2026. With Prabowo’s cabinet prioritizing food sovereignty, welfare expansion, and defense spending, IKN must justify every rupiah. OIKN head Basuki proposed Rp 15T for 2027 to accelerate legislative and judicial buildings.

Land Rights & Indigenous Community Issues

The IKN Law (UU No. 3/2022) has been criticized for insufficient Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC) for indigenous (adat) communities. The RUU Masyarakat Adat (Indigenous Peoples Bill) has been debated for 10+ years without passage. A positive step: OIKN Decree No. 84/2026 (May 2026) formally recognized the local wisdom of the Paser indigenous community in Mentawir.

Transport Setbacks & Smart City Gaps

The Autonomous Rail Transit (ART) units — China-made trackless trams — were returned to their manufacturer after 2024 Proof-of-Concept trials failed to achieve full autonomy, frequently requiring manual driver intervention. A revised BRT-focused transport masterplan was finalized in mid-2025. International Financial Center plans were also removed from IKN in July 2026, with Bali now considered as an alternative location.

Legal Status & Constitutional Uncertainty

A Constitutional Court ruling (Decision No. 71/PUU-XXIV/2026, May 2026) affirmed that Jakarta remains Indonesia’s legal capital until a Presidential Decree (Keppres) formally transfers the capital to Nusantara. This Keppres is targeted for 2028, contingent on all three branches of government (executive, legislative, judicial) being operationally present in IKN.

Future Concept

Vision 2045: The Smart Forest City

The long-term objectives designed to position IKN as a carbon-neutral city and a global model for sustainability.

🌱 Carbon Neutral & 100% Renewable

Achieve Net Zero Emissions by 2045. Solar arrays, wind turbines, and smart microgrids will power municipal functions, eliminating fossil fuels entirely.

🚲 10-Minute City Mobility

A layout where 80% of transits are done via electric buses, Autonomous Rail Transit (ART), walking, or cycling. Essential public services are reachable in a short walk.

🌐 100% Digital Integration

Smart governance platforms, unified IoT sensors to optimize energy and waste, and full fiber connectivity throughout the metropolitan areas.

🏆 Top-10 Global Liveability

Aiming to rank among the world's most liveable cities, blending active ecosystems, clean air, green jobs, and premium health networks.
