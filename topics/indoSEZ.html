Legislative & Institutional Architecture

The KEK program is not a marketing label — it is a layered legal instrument, rebuilt twice to close its own bureaucratic gaps.

The establishment of Special Economic Zones, legally designated as Kawasan Ekonomi Khusus (KEK), is a regulatory and macroeconomic mechanism used to attract foreign direct investment, accelerate industrial downstream processing (hilirisasi), and stimulate decentralized growth across the archipelago. The program's legal foundation began with Law No. 39 of 2009, was reinforced by PP No. 2 of 2011 and PP No. 96 of 2015, and was later folded into the Omnibus Law on Job Creation (Law No. 11 of 2020) alongside implementing regulations PP No. 1 of 2020 and PP No. 12 of 2020 — reforms that resentralized oversight and built the Online Single Submission (OSS) platform.

Governance runs through a four-tier public-private chain, from national strategy down to the operator physically building each site. Select a tier below to inspect its administrative mandate:

Dewan Nasional KEK — National Council
▸
Led by the Coordinating Minister for Economic Affairs. Sets strategy, evaluates annual performance, approves zone proposal submissions, and recommends establishment or revocation directly to the President.
Dewan Kawasan — Regional Councils
▸
Provincial-level bodies translating national policies into local implementation, handling regional infrastructure sync, and regional tax adjustments.
Administrator KEK
▸
One-stop public service interface operating physically inside each zone to process licensing, customs clearance, and environmental clearance instantly.
BUPP — Badan Usaha Pengelola
▸
The development and management body — state-owned, regional, or private enterprise — tasked with building site infrastructure, securing anchor tenants, and operating the physical zone day-to-day.

Fiscal incentives

  • CIT reductions / tax holidays up to 100% (up to 20 years based on capital size)
  • Import duty, VAT & luxury tax exemptions on capital goods and raw materials
  • Expedited customs green-channel clearance

Non-fiscal incentives

  • Specialized labor structures and simplified foreign-expert visa approvals
  • Long-term land titles with automatic pre-approved extensions
  • Foreign ownership allowance up to 100% in industrial and digital assets

Regulatory Constraints & Revocation Protocol

Under Article 9 of Law No. 39 of 2009, zones must achieve operational readiness within 36 months or face structural reconfiguration.

A KEK designation is a conditional legal mandate. Stagnant developments trigger systematic adjustments: boundary reallocations, operational extensions (subject to strict force majeure clauses), executive restructuring, or total status revocation.

01

Boundary Reductions

Re-demarcating the geographic footprint to isolate and protect active plots while reclaiming unutilized land allotments.

02

Operational Extension

Granting a one-time administrative grace period up to 24 months, contingent upon documented delays beyond developer control.

03

Operator Substitution

Replacing the managing BUPP entity with a new public-private syndicate or state enterprise to break developmental bottlenecks.

04

Status Revocation

Issuing a formal presidential decree to strip KEK classification, resulting in immediate termination of all custom and tax privileges.

Strategic Conclusions

KEKs succeed where fiscal incentives meet natural logistical advantages — deep ports, dense labor pools, stable grids — and stall wherever those preconditions are missing.

Java's and the strategic maritime-border zones — Gresik, Kendal, Galang Batang — prove the model works when incentives compound with real infrastructure, outperforming adjacent non-KEK regions on every major investment metric. The isolated outer-island tourism zones — Morotai, Tanjung Kelayang, Likupang — prove the inverse: tax breaks alone can't fix a missing flight route or a weak grid. Against the government's 8% growth target, the National Council's performance-based auditing model — phasing out stagnant projects, reallocating capital to zones that perform — is the mechanism actually doing the sorting.
