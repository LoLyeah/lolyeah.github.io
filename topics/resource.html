The Import Dependency Paradox

Despite being a historically significant oil producer, Indonesia became a net oil importer in 2004 and has not reversed this status. Domestic petroleum demand (29.91% of primary energy) persistently outpaces a declining production base, negatively impacting the national trade balance.

The strategic response is twofold: an aggressive Rp 543 Trillion upstream reinvestment plan (SKK Migas, 2024–2029) targeting 138 projects, and a parallel push into non-conventional CBM — attempting to synthesize the nation's vast coal wealth with its gas demand deficit.

East Kalimantan: The Coal Epicenter

East Kalimantan hosts the highest concentration of high-yield seams and the most prolific open-pit mining operations in the archipelago. The province accounts for 38.2% of national coal reserves (EIA 2024), with South Sumatra accounting for another 29.3%.

A structural paradox looms: as the Republic relocates its capital to Nusantara in East Kalimantan, the province must reconcile its identity as an extractive, high-emission coal hub with the federal vision of a sustainable green capital city.

Tin: The Bangka Belitung Monopoly

91% of Indonesia's ~800,000 ton tin reserves are in Bangka Belitung

Indonesia is the world's 2nd largest mined tin producer at ~23% of global output. Extraction uses offshore bucket-wheel dredges and Kapal Isap Produksi in the shallow Java Sea. 45,995 MT of refined tin was exported throughout 2024.

Provincial Verdict
The Most
Endowed Province

Determining the "most resource-rich" province requires bifurcating between legacy bulk volume and strategic forward value — two very different answers.

★ Strategic Future Value · #1
North Maluku
Maluku Utara · Nickel + REE Potential

Holds the highest volume of proven nickel ore reserves nationally — 1.86 billion tons of proven ore yielding 19.09 million tons of pure nickel metal, backed by 5.71 billion tons of additional resources. Home to the Weda Bay industrial complex and the linchpin of Indonesia's EV battery supply chain. Its geological dominance over nickel — the single most critical mineral of the current decade — provides exponentially more geopolitical leverage than any other single province.

◈ Absolute Physical Volume · #1
East Kalimantan
Kalimantan Timur · Coal Hegemon

By sheer geological mass and total thermal energy potential, entirely peerless. The epicenter of Indonesia's 31.95 billion ton proven coal inventory — dwarfing any other mineral commodity in excavated mass. Undisputed engine of the domestic electricity grid and national trade balance. However, faces long-term stranded-asset risk from global decarbonization and a profound structural tension as the new national capital Nusantara takes shape within its borders.

01
Permanent geographic pivot eastward. The center of extractive wealth has irreversibly shifted from aging western oilfields and depleted tin mines toward deepwater gas basins (Maluku/Papua), porphyry copper-gold systems (Papua/NTB), and vast lateritic nickel belts (Sulawesi/North Maluku).
02
The green paradox. Indonesia's ambition to dominate the clean energy transition through nickel, copper, and REEs remains paradoxically dependent on East Kalimantan's coal — the very fuel powering the HPAL and RKEF smelters processing those transition metals. Maluku and Papua's gas reserves must ultimately serve as the bridge to a cleaner industrial baseload.
03
Downstreaming as sovereign doctrine. The hilirisasi mandate — enforced through raw ore export bans on nickel (2020) and bauxite (2023) — has successfully redirected billions in global FDI into domestic processing. Total national exports are projected to reach $1,345.50 billion by 2025, driven by high-value battery precursors over cheap raw ore.
04
REE development as the final puzzle piece. The 8 designated REE blocks across Kalimantan, Sulawesi, and Bangka Belitung represent Indonesia's bid for full-spectrum vertical integration in permanent magnet manufacturing. With a 7–10 year horizon and $2–3B CAPEX requirement, this remains speculative but strategically decisive for the post-2030 landscape.
