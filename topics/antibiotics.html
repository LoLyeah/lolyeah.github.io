Critical Priority Resistant Pathogens (WHO 2024)

Newest & Emerging Agents

Novel antibiotics approved 2015–2025, targeting resistant organisms where classical antibiotics fail. Many exploit new mechanisms or β-lactamase inhibitor (BLI) combinations.

2015
Ceftazidime-Avibactam
Avycaz · AstraZeneca/Pfizer
A cephalosporin + novel non-β-lactam BLI (avibactam) combination. Inhibits KPC, OXA-48, and AmpC β-lactamases. Key treatment for KPC-producing Klebsiella and difficult Pseudomonas where meropenem has failed.
BLI Combo KPC · OXA-48 · AmpC Non-β-lactam BLI
2015
Ceftolozane-Tazobactam
Zerbaxa · Merck
Novel cephalosporin with enhanced affinity for Pseudomonas PBPs, combined with tazobactam BLI. Specifically developed for MDR and XDR Pseudomonas aeruginosa, particularly in ventilator-associated pneumonia and UTI settings.
PBP Binding MDR Pseudomonas
2018
Meropenem-Vaborbactam
Vabomere · Melinta
Carbapenem + cyclic boronate BLI combination. Vaborbactam potently inhibits KPC serine carbapenemases while meropenem retains its broad-spectrum activity. Approved for KPC-producing carbapenem-resistant Enterobacterales (CRE).
Boronate BLI KPC-CRE Cyclic Boronate
2019
Imipenem-Cilastatin-Relebactam
Recarbrio · Merck
Imipenem-cilastatin enhanced with relebactam, a new diazabicyclooctane (DBO) BLI that inhibits class A and C β-lactamases including KPC. Approved for hospital-acquired and ventilator-associated bacterial pneumonia and complicated UTI caused by susceptible organisms.
DBO BLI KPC · AmpC Diazabicyclooctane
2019
Cefiderocol
Fetroja · Shionogi
A siderophore cephalosporin — exploits bacterial iron-uptake pathways to deliver high concentrations into the periplasm, bypassing outer membrane impermeability. Active against pan-drug-resistant gram-negatives including MBL-producing organisms where all other β-lactams fail. Notable activity against Stenotrophomonas maltophilia.
Siderophore Trojan Horse MBL · CR-Acinetobacter · XDR Iron-Chelation Uptake
2019
Omadacycline
Nuzyra · Paratek
A first-in-class aminomethylcycline tetracycline with enhanced binding to 30S ribosomes. Overcomes both tetracycline efflux pumps and ribosomal protection. Oral and IV formulations. Approved for CAP and ABSSSI including MRSA, atypicals, and tetracycline-resistant organisms.
Aminomethylcycline MRSA · Atypicals · CABP Enhanced 30S Binding
2020
Lefamulin
Xenleta · Nabriva
First systemic pleuromutilin antibiotic approved for humans. Inhibits protein synthesis via a unique binding site in the 50S ribosomal peptidyl transferase center — distinct from other ribosomal antibiotics. IV and oral forms for community-acquired bacterial pneumonia (CABP) including MRSA, S. pneumoniae, Mycoplasma, and Legionella.
First Systemic Pleuromutilin CABP · MRSA · Atypicals PTC Binding
2024–25
Zoliflodacin & Gepotidacin
Zoliflodacin (Entasis) · Gepotidacin (GSK/Haleon) — GEPOTAZ
Two novel antibiotics in late-stage development / recently approved targeting N. gonorrhoeae and UTIs. Zoliflodacin: first-in-class spiropyrimidinetrione, novel topoisomerase II inhibitor with no cross-resistance to fluoroquinolones. Gepotidacin: triazaacenaphthylene, also inhibits DNA gyrase via a distinct mechanism — effective against fluoroquinolone-resistant and ESBL-producing uropathogens.
First-in-Class Spiropyrimidinetrione XDR Gonorrhea · UTI Novel GyrB/ParE Binding
