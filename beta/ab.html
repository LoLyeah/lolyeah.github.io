Cellular Architecture & Morphology

This section establishes the foundational differences between Gram-positive, Gram-negative, and Mycobacterial cell structures. Understanding the physical barriers of these pathogens is critical, as the cellular envelope dictates intrinsic antibiotic resistance and determines which therapeutic agents can successfully penetrate and neutralize the bacteria.

Gram-Positive Organisms

Characterized by a thick, cross-linked peptidoglycan layer that provides structural integrity but remains relatively porous to small molecules. Without an outer membrane, they are historically more susceptible to beta-lactam antibiotics that disrupt cell wall synthesis.

Common Pathogens

Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) Streptococcus pneumoniae Enterococcus faecalis Clostridium difficile

Primary Resistance Mechanisms

  • Target modification (e.g., PBP2a in MRSA)
  • Enzymatic degradation (beta-lactamases)
  • Target structural bypass (Vancomycin resistance in VRE)
